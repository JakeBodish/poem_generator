
You that do search for every purling spring Which from the ribs of old Parnassus flows And every flower not sweet perhaps which grows Near thereabouts into your poesy wring Ye that do dictionarys method bring Into your rimes running in rattling rows You that poor Petrarchs long-deceased woes With new-born sighs and denizend wit do sing You take wrong ways those far-fet helps be such As do bewray a want of inward touch And sure at length stoln goods do come to light  But if both for your love and skill your name You seek to nurse at fullest breasts of Fame Stella behold and then begin to endite
