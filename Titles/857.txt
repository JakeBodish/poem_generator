
Look Delia how we steem the half-blown roseThe image of thy blush and summers honourWhilst in her tender green she doth encloseThat pure sweet beauty time bestows upon her No sooner spreads her glory in the airBut straight her full-blown pride is in decliningShe then is scornd that late adornd the fairSo clouds thy beauty after fairest shining No April can revive thy witherd flowersWhose blooming grace adorns thy beauty nowSwift speedy time featherd with flying hoursDissolves the beauty of the fairest brow O let not then such riches waste in vainBut love whilst that thou mayst be lovd again
