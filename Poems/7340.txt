
Avising the bright beams of these fair eyesWhere he is that mine oft moisteth and washethThe wearied mind straight from the heart departethFor to rest in his worldly paradiseAnd find the sweet bitter under this guise What webs he hath wrought well he perceivethWhereby with himself on love he plainethThat spurreth with fire and bridleth with ice Thus is it in such extremity broughtIn frozen thought now and now it standeth in flame Twixt misery and wealth twixt earnest and gameBut few glad and many diverse thoughtWith sore repentance of his hardiness Of such a root cometh fruit fruitless
