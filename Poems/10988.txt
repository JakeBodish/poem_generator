
Teach me old World your passion of slow change    Your calm of stars watching the turn of earthPatient of man and never thinking strange    The mad red crash of each new system’s birth Teach me for I would know your beauty’s way     That waits and changes with each changing sunNo dawn so fair but promises a day    Of other perfectness than men have won Teach me old World not as vain men have taught     Unpatient song nor words of hollow brassNor men’s dismay whose powerfullest thought    Is woe that they and worlds alike must pass Nothing I learn by any mortal ruleTeach me old World I would not be man’s fool
