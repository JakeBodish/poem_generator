
  A poet He hath put his heart to schoolNor dares to move unpropped upon the staffWhich art hath lodged within his hand must laughBy precept only and shed tears by rule Thy Art be Nature the live current quaffAnd let the groveller sip his stagnant poolIn fear that else when Critics grave and coolHave killed him Scorn should write his epitaph How does the Meadow-flower its bloom unfoldBecause the lovely little flower is freeDown to its root and in that freedom boldAnd so the grandeur of the Forest-treeComes not by casting in a formal mouldBut from its own divine vitality
